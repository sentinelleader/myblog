2012 Goals

January 12th, 2012
life

Instead of the plain old yearly goals that everyone outlines for the future, every new year, I started thinking about themes and what means the most to me in 2012.  What can I change now, starting now, today.  I have come to understand and accept my past, learn from it, grow from it, but not let it hold me back.  I am not afraid to tell others what I feel, even if it's not what they want to hear.  I have grown to accept the fact that pleasing everyone is truly impossible, and being a chameleon is b.s.   When you set out to start a company, sail a ship, buy a one way ticket you have to have a set of parameters to guide you, yet, not restrict you so much there are no alternatives.  Since I have left Chicago, life has been a roller coaster to say the least.  There have been ups and downs, but I still do not regret taking my first steps on the path away from where I grew up.  The nervousness has since subsided, but the excitement hasn't.  The friends I have met on my journey outside of Chicago have quickly become part of the chosen family I hope to have forever, and the plans I have made with the love of my life Kathryn warm my heart.  So what's next? I think of this all the time.  Yes, there are many things brewing in the kitchen - have to say this for our stalkers.  But focusing on themes, I have locked in three for me:Think, Create, Act. Yes, they are a little broad, but after quite a few sessions on the treadmill I was able to narrow down my focus to these three.

Think

This year I want to dive deeper into Buddhism, learn more about the spiritual journey, it's a piece of me that I have grown to appreciate the more I dive in.  Directly related to this spiritual initiative is what I call being more "zen" or the "water flowing through rocks."  Working and living in a major city like Chicago or Manhattan, comes with stress, plain and simple. If you have ever lived in a major city, you know what I am referring to.  No, I am not going into the rants now, I will save them for later, but they are easy to list.  While you can not control others, you have full control over your reactions to others' behaviors or lack there of.  I have come to learn and actively practice not letting much get to me.  Life is just too short to be stressed right?  If you follow me on twitter, you just read this and was like "yeah right" but acknowledge this, there is a big difference between rants {tweets} and the movements you make every day in life just walking down the street, holding the door for your elderly neighbor, and helping a child get a drink off the coffee shop counter.

Create

I enjoy working in coldfusion, but to be honest. I am creative.  I have started learning Ruby and Rails this year and have been focusing more on UIE.  As my wife has taken the leap into learning how to code, I will be spending time  on a journey I call back to the basics.  I am planning on going through the same set of Treehouse tasks she has been going through.  I figure a solid refresher, no matter how long you have been coding can't hurt.  Plus, there are always things that you have been doing, sometimes for years, incorrectly in terms of coding architecture and principles.  I want to write more in 2012, not for you, but for me.  While I would welcome an audience to read my craziness, I want to be open with myself, be authentic, and put it out there on paper.  Don't care if I get signed for a book.  It's for me. Enjoy.

Act

I have made a commitment to kill off my fat stomach.  No, I do not have body image issues, but health and fitness are huge priorities in my life.  I like being tone, fit, and that means it has to go.  Luckily we live a very holistic lifestyle and are probably one of the only families who actually cook at home in Manhattan.  While I may never get a 6 pack, shedding about ten pounds would make me very happy.  Being married to a personal trainer has it's perks and have already put on a significant amount of muscle mass breaking through markers on squats, shoulder presses, and even simple things like dips.  Yet, being the type-a person I am, I have quite a ways to go.

I will expand on these as make my way through this year, and to be honest with you, this year's plans already make me beyond excited.  What do you think? Think I am crazy?  I hope you do.  Keep on fighting, keep on learning, keep on kicking ass and taking names.
