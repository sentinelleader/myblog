I run 4 the Fuck Yous & Fuck Yeahs!

October 23rd, 2011
life

What do you think of when you run? When you’re busting your ass at the gym? Is it only about losing weight or something more?  My journey to health started a few years ago when I landed in Colorado.  I used to weigh over 200lbs! Yes, I know.  Fat ass! Seriously though, it was pretty disgusting, and if you ever visit Colorado, you’ll learn quickly why it’s known as the healthiest state in the U.S.

When I met my wife she encouraged me to start training, working out for more than just weight loss, and instead focusing on events.  Not competing to win, but running to be part of the small percentage of like minded bad asses that swim, bike, run for fun.  The same drive and determination to succeed in life easily applies to racing, to exercise, to anything for that matter.  After deep periods of reflection in my life I came to discover that I always did everything, every sport, every task, whatever 110%.  That means if you sat a bottle of Kettle One in front of me, consider it gone. Type-A people don’t fuck around, they don’t waste time.  They need a mission, a goal to hook on to, to run with, to put that high paced energy into.  Often, they choose poorly.  I chose to be an athlete.

When I first started running, I used to picture all of the people, places, events that pissed me off.  You know the ones right?  I pictured them as though I was there, tasting the air, feeling the emotions rushing through my veins. In my mind, I was punching that person in the face, standing up in front of haters and screaming fuck you, driving that ferrari 360 modena top down at 150mph.  Working out angry solved a problem. It seemed to fill a void.  The rush of adrenaline helped kill off the old feelings, the baggage I had been carrying.

As an athlete you can either work out angry or happy.  I only knew how to run for the fuck yous.  More and more time went by, and I shaved more and more time off my current mile pace as well as built a good amount of new muscle mass, yet, something was missing.  I was losing energy.  The anger wasn’t there anymore.  One day running on the treadmill my ipod started blasting “I Remember”, by Kaskade.

I felt a new rush come over me that I had never felt before.  It was pure happiness.  I wasn’t mad anymore.  This newfound energy rushed through my veins even more powerful than when I was angry. I felt lighter, less burdened, and the thoughts rushing through my mind were happy, thankful, filled with joy.  I started running for the fuck yeahs.

The people, the events, the experiences I am grateful for in life.  The times I cried out of joy, the plans I have for the future, the focus I have in my journey of enlightenment.  Living.  I know nothing is perfect in life, but what I have learned so far, is that being negative, carrying anger, being mad, just wastes time.  It brings negative energy into your life and the lives of those around you.  Yes, running angry for me served a purpose, a means to purge myself of older lingering feelings, to get rid of fear, to make room for the holes to be filled with light.  If you’re working out angry, be conscious of these feelings, let them pass, and work towards working out happy.  Be yourself, and let these feelings go, move on to better things.

Today is Monday, October 24th 2011, and this is my first ever blog post.  If you do not like what I have to say, so be it.  Go somewhere else.  I don’t care if you like it or not, my writing is not for everyone.  If you care to share what inspires you to run, I would love to find out.  I hope we can inspire each other.
